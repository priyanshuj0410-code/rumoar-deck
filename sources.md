# RUMOAR — Deck Sources

Numbered to match the `[n]` citation markers on the slides. On the live HTML deck each `[n]` is interactive: **external sources open in a new tab; our own observations, model and assumptions (`[Obs]`/`[A]`) open a short logic popover** (there is no outside page to send you to, so the reasoning is shown inline).

**Evidence tiers:** `[F]` filed / official · `[N]` named publication or research firm · `[C]` company / creator / supplier-claimed · `[Obs]` our own primary observation (dated) · `[A]` our labelled assumption or model output.

**Verification note.** Every external figure was checked against a primary or named source before entering the deck, and figures that could not be verified were removed or softened rather than published: the "share of purchases chosen by someone else" stat was dropped (unverifiable); Snitch is shown at its **filed** FY25 revenue (₹498 Cr), not the founder-stated FY26 figure; Thread's raise is stated as **$40M+** (M&S/Retail Gazette) rather than a lower round figure; StyleCracker's precise recent revenue was dropped as unverifiable; SALTY's "25–30%" is labelled as its **men's product line's** share of business, not a customer count. Two links are deliberately *representative* of a bundle and flagged as such (13, 22).
